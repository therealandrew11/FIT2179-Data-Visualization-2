Australian Beef Cattle Distribution and Change

Spatial and temporal patterns in beef cattle populations across Australian states and territories (2019–20 to 2024–25). The dashboard combines geographic distribution with year-on-year growth trends.

Key insights

  • Queensland dominates national herd size across all observed years.
  • Most regions show growth up to 2023–24, followed by partial decline.
  • Southern states exhibit stronger volatility than northern grazing regions.
  • Recent downturn aligns with destocking and weaker seasonal conditions.

Context

Beef cattle numbers in Australia follow cyclical patterns driven by rainfall, pasture availability, and herd rebuilding after drought periods. Changes in herd size do not directly correspond to production output in the same year.
