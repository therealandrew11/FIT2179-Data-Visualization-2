Australian Beef Cattle Distribution and Change

Spatial and temporal patterns in beef cattle populations across Australian states and territories (2019–20 to 2024–25).

Key insights

  • Queensland dominates national herd size across all years.
  • Most regions grow until 2023–24, then soften.
  • Northern systems are more stable than southern regions.
  • Recent decline aligns with destocking conditions.

Context

Australian beef cattle systems are strongly climate-dependent and follow multi-year herd cycles driven by rainfall and pasture availability.
